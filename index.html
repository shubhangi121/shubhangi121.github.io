How I
think.

01

Find the gap no one's looking at

The most impactful work is often invisible until someone maps it. I go to where the friction actually lives.

26 factories existed in the data. No one had looked. $4M of orders got better margins the next season.

02

Test beyond QA sign-off

Formal testing catches what the test plan covers. DITL testing catches what the business actually does.

12,000 bad data rows headed to SAP, caught after QA signed off. The LLD data-mapping tab is now standard practice.

03

Reintroduce the right idea at the right time

Good ideas rejected early aren't dead; they're waiting for the right stakeholder or context.

Reason code restriction rejected once. Reintroduced under a new manager. 30% fewer plan date updates. Adopted by the SAP team.

04

Docs people actually use

A 40-page training manual creates dependency. A well-structured Quick Reference Guide creates autonomy.

Queries dropped from 6/day to 1–2/month with a single concise QRG, not a heavy training manual.

05

Earn the strategy seat

I don't wait for permission to think strategically. Insight + data + cross-functional trust earns the seat.

Team started pulling me into Chase Order strategy, not just system change requirement discussions.

06

Own the outcome, not just the delivery

Shipping is a milestone. Adoption, iteration, and measurable reduction in pain, that's the goal.

90%+ adoption and a testing methodology now standardised across all workspace launches.
